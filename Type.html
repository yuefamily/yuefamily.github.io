Traditional Butter Tarts:
These are the classic and most well-known butter tarts. They consist of a flaky pastry shell filled with a sweet and gooey filling made primarily from butter, sugar (often brown sugar or a mix of brown and white sugar), eggs, and sometimes a small amount of corn syrup or maple syrup. Traditional butter tarts have a rich, caramel-like flavor and a slightly runny center when baked.
Raisin Butter Tarts:
This variation of butter tarts includes raisins added to the filling. The debate over whether or not to include raisins in butter tarts is a long-standing one in Canada. Some people love the added sweetness and texture that raisins bring, while others prefer their butter tarts without them.
Pecan Butter Tarts:
Pecan butter tarts include pecan nuts added to the filling.
The combination of pecans with the sweet and buttery filling adds a delightful crunch and nutty flavor to the tarts.
Walnut Butter Tarts:
Similar to pecan butter tarts, these tarts contain walnuts in the filling.
The walnuts provide a distinct flavor and texture.
Chocolate Butter Tarts:
This variation includes chocolate chips or chunks added to the filling.
The melted chocolate blends with the buttery, sugary filling to create a deliciously indulgent treat.
Coconut Butter Tarts:
Coconut butter tarts incorporate shredded coconut into the filling.
The coconut adds a subtle tropical flavor and texture contrast.
Maple Butter Tarts:
In this variation, maple syrup is a prominent ingredient in the filling, giving the tarts a strong maple flavor.
Maple butter tarts are particularly popular in regions of Canada where maple syrup is abundant.
Nut-Free Butter Tarts:
Some people have nut allergies, so nut-free versions of butter tarts omit nuts, making them safe for those with allergies while still retaining the classic butter tart flavor.
